■ WHAT IS THISreadme.txt

The web used to be made of people instead of feeds.

A webring is a loop of independent sites that link to each other in a circle. You land on one, hit next, and end up in some stranger's corner of the internet, maybe a blog, maybe a portfolio, maybe a pile of half-finished experiments. Hit it again and you're somewhere else, until eventually you loop back to where you started. Nothing here gets ranked or tracked, and no algorithm decides what you get to see.

Every link goes to a room someone built by hand.

✎ JOIN THE RINGinvite only

This ring is invite-only. You can't add yourself. Someone already in the ring has to vouch for you, and an admin merges you in. If a member sent you here, you're most of the way there. If nobody did, go build a site worth inviting.

Starting the ring? The very first member is the founder, so there's no one to ask yet. Add yourself with invitedBy set to "founder" and you're in without an invite. Everyone after you needs a vouch.

Step 1. Got an invite? Fork the repo and add your entry to webring.json. Set invitedBy to the handle of the member who vouched for you, and leave your entry at the end of the list:

{
  "name":      "your-handle",
  "gh":        "your-github-username",
  "url":       "https://your-site.com",
  "invitedBy": "member-who-invited-you"
}

Step 2. Put the ring navigation on your own site so visitors can keep moving. Type your handle to build your snippet:

Open a pull request and ping the member who invited you. An automated check stays red until they approve your PR with their own GitHub account, so nobody can sneak in by typing a name. Once they approve, an admin merges you in. Full instructions live in JOIN.md.
